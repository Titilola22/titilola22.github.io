How it happens: Who are the victims of sexual violence?

Most sexual assault occured mostly in the assailant's house or office(48.5%).females of less than twenty were majorly victims.Sexual assualt through vaginal route only(87.7%)was the most common route of sexual assault. Threat of violence(31.1%) and physical physical force(29.6%) was the common method for overcoming the victims.

what are the consequences ?

The effect of sexual violence is always on the victims, though it affect the victims family and friends as well as society as a whole.in this regards ,sexual assault is a public health problem that concern everyone. Sexual assault has numerous potential consequences that can last a lifetime and span generations, with serious adverse effects on health, education, crime and the economic well-being of individuals, families, communities and societies. Victim of child abuse can face immediate psychological consequences as well as chronic effects that can impact their adjustment throughout their development.Such as emotional regulation,post traumatic stress disorder ,cognitive style and coping mechanisms and thus entail long-term consequencies

what help do we render

No statistics on sxual violence provide a complete picture of the problem or its prevalence.shame, fear, stigmatisation and many other obstacles prevent an unknown number of victims from receiving ,or even seekig treatment. And yet getting immediate medical care after sexual assault is critical in order to limit the potential consequences. our team are here to treat victims of sexual violence.we provide phychotherapy for survivors of sexual violence. We provide a safe and supportive environment to augment the process of healing from the sexual assault. Our proffessional counsellors offer therapy for trauma received from childhood sexual assault or adult survivors of sexual violence. Various types of psychotherapy and cognitive processing therapies are offered to assist the victim in the trauma healing process.

What to expect from our Therapist

Your counsellor may ask you to keep a thought and dream journal where you record any thoughts that comes up about the sexual assault/violence during your everyday activities.This is because certain environmental triggers can set off the negative thought patterns associated with trauma assault. By reviewing your journal, the therapist can gain insight into these triggers and develop a strategy to remove their detrimental effect on your thought patterns.

Timelines-

Some people are concerned that starting therapy means entering into a life long contact. That isn't usually the case. while there is no timline for recovering from sexual assault or abue, you may be able to work with a therapist for a defined amount of time to help you find ways to heal from the experience.
